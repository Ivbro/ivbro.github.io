Calmscape

Back to all projects

Emergency rooms in hospitals are often busy and chaotic places. Most people are in pain or discomfort and want to be helped as quickly as possible. However, even though some conditions are very unpleasant, not all require immediate attention. Combined with limited staff and capacity, patients often experience extended waiting times, which is often very stressful. This is why we have created the Calmscape.
‍
The Calmscape is a physical artwork that can be placed in waiting rooms of hospitals. Scenes of natures are shown to help calm people and actuators within the design allow the canvas to move subtly. These small movements help to draw attention to the artwork and maintain it, distracting the patients from their discomfort and other patients waiting in the emergency room. Testing with the Calmscape has shown that it both has a relaxing and distracting effect on users.
‍
Learning points:
‍
The main focus of this project was exploration during the early stages of the design process, as well as learning about working with different methodologies. I helped me get a firm grasp on different design methodologies, as well as adapting them to your own specific needs. However, I also realized that this early phase of the design process, especially without a clear context, does not have my preference. The project was matched to a specific context and problem roughly midway, however in hindsight this was not an effective approach. The project would have worked better as a structured exploration and should have been framed as such.
