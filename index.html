Hi, I am Ivo Léon, BSc Industrial Design graduate and current MSc ID student at Eindhoven University of Technology. Furthermore, I am a designer specializing user research & evaluation, as well as designing and prototyping both physical and digital products.
As a designer I aim to create impactful products and services that match innovation to user needs and requirements. I find it important to design with vision and create products that provide value to the end user. I do this through three important principles: Empathy, Curiosity and Flexibility. Empathy helps to understand the user and create meaningful products. Curiosity helps to take a deep dive into the design and context, to find out what is truly important when designing a product or system. Flexibility helps to adapt to the often changing landscape of design and utilize design processes that fit the specific project. These principles, among many other smaller ones, guide my design activities.
During the past 4+ years of my study I have completed a wide range of design projects, of which a selection is presented below. Please take a look and feel free to contact me in case of questions or interest.

Artifical Womb

The Artificial Womb is a life support system for extreme premature babies. When babies are born 22 to 24 weeks into the pregnancy their chance of survival is very low. Therefore the artificial womb aims to replace neonatal incubators and mimic the conditions in the uterus, to provide the highest survival rate and minimize negative side effects of premature birth.
The starting point for this project was a range of research papers proving that the artificial womb was possible, which detailed the requirements. This information, together with expert interviews and user research was translated to the first real design of the artificial womb, which was in turn improved over multiple iterations.
The goal of this project was not to design a product that is market-ready, due to the sheer complexity of the subject. However, it embodies the current knowledge and provides a concept-design that functions as a stepping stone for further development and research.

Calmscape

Emergency rooms in hospitals are often busy & chaotic places. Most people are in pain or discomfort and want to be helped as quickly as possible. However, even though some conditions are very unpleasant, not all require immediate attention. Combined with limited staff and capacity, patients are often waiting for extended times, which is often very stressful. This is why we have created the Calmscape.
The Calmscape is a physical artwork that is to be placed in waiting rooms of hospitals. Scenes of natures are shown to help calm people and actuators within the design allow the canvas to move subtly. These small movements help to draw attention to the artwork and maintain it, distracting the patients from their discomfort and other patients waiting in the emergency room. Testing with the Calmscape has shown that it both has a relaxing and distracting effect on users.

Stress Management Research

This research project looked at stress management practices. Stress can have large effect on both mental and physical health. Extensive research has shown that a wide range of health conditions can arise from stress, specifically chronic stress. One possible complication is Cardio Vascular Disease (CVD). CVD patients are especially vulnerable to reoccurring complications and therefore effective stress management is vitally important. Based on this a large scale research project at the TU/e is being conducted, focusing on monitoring different lifestyle factors of CVD patients, including stress levels.
From this my own research project has stemmed. The platform itself only provides insight into the stress levels of patients and does not provide any guidance. At the same time a large amount of stress management techniques exist. This prompted me to compare stress awareness and stress management technique usage between 2 groups: one group that only measured their stress levels and received this as feedback and the second group was stimulated to engage in stress management based on behavior change models.
To conduct this research I have created a digital prototype in the form a website, which was both able to provide the participants with the measurements and persuasive messages as well allow them to record the data that I needed to analyze the results. The results show to that intervention usage did not significantly increase for both groups. However, the stress awareness did significantly increase for the measurement group.

Ambient Notificator

During one of my project courses we have designed a new way to display notifications, with the aim to make digital connectivity less intrusive. The design process was mainly focused on using crowdsourcing platforms, online communities and online workers to aid in the creation of the design, as these can be very powerful tools.
The project started with a need-finding phase, where online workers (through Prolific & M-Turk) were asked to complete a survey to study the problems and opportunities related to the subject of notifications and digital connectivity. Based on the results a new group of workers was asked to come up with concepts. These concepts were then filtered by us, and presented for online evaluation. The best received concept was then further iterated upon by us, to finally be sent to a digital artist to visualize the concept. The final concept took the form of a painting/artwork, which would change scene when the user would receive a notification. Albeit it being a luxury item, it provides a subtle way to display notifications, without interrupting the user and maintaining privacy of the content of the notifications.

Increasing Fitness Guidance

The last project that I have completed targets people that practice fitness within a gym. Fitness has a range of both physical and mental benefits and is very widely practiced with the industry growing larger every day. With the low entry barrier, due to low costs and abundant gyms available it is practiced by many.
However, there are also problems that arise within this context. Based on explorative interviews several themes arose, including problems with intimidation, occupation of equipment, form, beginner guidance and motivation. Further analysis through user journey mapping and a Co-Design session has steered the project towards a focus on guiding people that just start out with fitness. It was shown that a barrier is often experienced when asking for help, especially with beginners. Therefore a technical prototype was developed, designed specifically for easy integration in the gym with the aim of evaluation. The system consists of a small box with a button, which can be used to ask for assistance. It is linked via Wifi to a website that notifies the trainers. The system is focused on a simplistic external design, in order to make it easy to use and decrease the barrier for asking trainers for help as much as possible.
