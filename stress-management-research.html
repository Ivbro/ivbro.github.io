Evaluating Stress Management Practices

Back to all projects

This research project looked at stress management practices, as stress can have large effect on both mental and physical health. Research shows that especially chronic stress can result in a wide range of health conditions, such as cardiovascular disease. These patients are also vulnerable to reoccurring complications, making stress management vitally important.
‍
As platforms use both insight into stress and stress management techniques, a study was set-up to compare the effectiveness of both through A/B testing. A website was built as a digital prototype, where participants could record their stress levels. This also provided one group with an overview of their stress levels and stimulated the other group to engage in stress management based on behavior change models.
‍
The results show that intervention (stress management techniques) usage did not significantly increase for both groups. However, the stress awareness did significantly increase for group that had gotten insight into their stress levels.
‍
Learning points:
‍
This project focused on creating a high-fidelity digital prototype for conducting research. Furthermore, it focused on a dedication to quality, by ensuring statistically significant results from the study. The digital prototype proved to be extremely useful, which further developed my interest in the design of both digital and physical products. When looking back at the project I would have included more pilot tests and smaller trials before conducting larger scale studies, as this would have further improved the quality.
